\section{Data \& Methodology}

\subsection{Research Objective}

This analysis investigates the representation of Odisha Right to Public Services (ORTPS) in the state's grievance redressal system. We address two key questions:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Do ORTPS-covered services appear as distinct categories in citizen complaints submitted through the grievance redressal portal?
    \item If so, what issues do complaints related to these services typically address?
\end{enumerate}

Understanding ORTPS representation in grievance data can reveal service delivery bottlenecks, inform resource allocation decisions, and identify priority areas for process improvements.

\subsection{Methodological Challenge}

The primary challenge in identifying ORTPS-related complaints lies in the mismatch between administrative categorization and service-based classification. The existing complaint category system was not designed with the ORTPS framework in mind---categories reflect organizational structure (departments, divisions) rather than specific public services delivered to citizens.

For example, a complaint about a delayed caste certificate might be categorized under the Education category administratively, since a caste certificate may be needed to avail reserved seats in colleges and universities, but from a service delivery perspective, it falls under the ORTPS framework's identity documentation services. This misalignment necessitates an alternative approach to identify ORTPS-related complaints based on the complaint text itself rather than pre-assigned categories.

\subsection{ORTPS Category Consolidation}

We began with the full list of ORTPS-covered services as provided by CAGR and consolidated them into 10 issue-based categories covering major service domains. This consolidation balances specificity with analytical tractability, grouping related services that citizens and administrators perceive as similar types of requests.

The 10 consolidated categories are:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Identity \& Social Certificates} --- Caste certificates (SC/ST/OBC/SEBC), birth and death certificates, marriage registration, legal heir certificates

    \item \textbf{Income \& Welfare Benefits} --- Income certificates, Income \& Asset certificates (EWS), scholarships (pre-matric, post-matric, national/merit/minority schemes), ration cards (AAY, BPL, PHH, food security)

    \item \textbf{Land Records} --- Certified copies of Record of Rights (RoR), encumbrance certificates

    \item \textbf{Land Transactions} --- Property registration documents, certified copies of registered documents, mortgage permissions, conveyance deeds

    \item \textbf{Land Use Changes} --- Disposal of uncontested mutation cases, mutation orders for leasehold land, land conversion under OLR Act Section 8, partition of land under OLR Act Section 19

    \item \textbf{Building \& Construction} --- Building plan approvals by Urban Local Bodies (ULBs)/development authorities, permissions for building additions/alterations, fire safety recommendations and certificates

    \item \textbf{Utilities \& Connections} --- Piped water connections (BMC, CMC, BEMC), new electricity connections (non-industrial)

    \item \textbf{Driving Licences} --- Issuance of driving licences, licence renewals, change of address in driving licence, learner's licences

    \item \textbf{Vehicle Services} --- Certified copies of vehicle registration certificates (RC), transfer of vehicle ownership

    \item \textbf{Verification \& Legal} --- Employee/character/antecedent verification requests, certified copies of FIR, trade licences (provisional and final)
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Text-Based Classification Pipeline}

Given the administrative categorization challenge, we developed a two-stage automated classification pipeline operating directly on complaint text.

\subsubsection{Language Detection}

To ensure reliable keyword matching, we first filter complaints to English-language text using a two-stage detection approach:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Script Detection:} A regex-based filter identifies and excludes complaints containing Odia or Devanagari (Hindi) scripts, which are detectable by their Unicode character ranges.

    \item \textbf{Lingua Confidence Scoring:} For complaints passing the script filter, we use the Lingua language detection library to compute English-language confidence scores. Complaints with confidence $\geq 0.85$ are classified as English and retained for further analysis.
\end{enumerate}

This two-stage approach balances precision and recall, filtering out non-English text while preserving code-mixed complaints where English is dominant.

\subsubsection{Category Labeling}

For English-language complaints, we employ a hybrid classification approach combining keyword matching and semantic similarity:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Keyword Matching:} Case-insensitive substring matching against category-specific keyword lists. For example, ``Income \& Welfare Benefits'' includes keywords such as \textit{income certificate}, \textit{scholarship}, \textit{post matric}, \textit{ration card}, \textit{bpl card}, and related terms. This method captures complaints with explicit mentions of service names.

    \item \textbf{Embedding Similarity:} Complaints not matched by keywords are evaluated using sentence embeddings (all-MiniLM-L6-v2 model) to compute semantic similarity to category prototypes. Complaints with cosine similarity $\geq 0.45$ to any category are assigned to that category. This method captures semantically related complaints that may not use exact service terminology (e.g., ``need money certificate for college admission'' $\rightarrow$ Income \& Welfare Benefits).
\end{enumerate}

Complaints matching neither method remain unlabeled, indicating they likely concern non-ORTPS services (e.g., infrastructure, law and order, general administration).

\subsection{Data Scope}

We restrict our analysis along two dimensions:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Temporal scope:} July 2023 onwards (fiscal years 2023--2025). This recent time window reflects current service delivery patterns and ensures sufficient data volume for category-level analysis.

    \item \textbf{Language filter:} English-only complaints. Since our ORTPS keyword lists were developed in English, language filtering ensures classification reliability. While this excludes Odia-language complaints, English remains the predominant language in the portal (over 70\% of total complaints).
\end{itemize}

The final analysis dataset comprises \textbf{763,237 English-language complaints} from July 2023 onwards.

\section{Results: ORTPS Representation}

\input{fiscal_year_aggregation_wide.tex}

Of 763,237 English-language complaints analyzed, \textbf{71,223 complaints (9.3\%)} were identified as ORTPS-related using our classification pipeline.

This relatively low match rate reflects the focused, service-specific nature of ORTPS. The grievance portal captures a broad range of citizen concerns---infrastructure quality, law and order issues, general administrative queries, and non-ORTPS government services---of which ORTPS-covered services constitute one important but bounded subset.

The 9.3\% match rate should be interpreted as indicating:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Focused categorization:} Our classification correctly identifies a well-defined subset of complaints rather than over-classifying unrelated complaints as ORTPS-related.

    \item \textbf{Service portfolio breadth:} Government service delivery extends well beyond ORTPS-covered services. Many complaints concern services outside ORTPS scope (e.g., infrastructure maintenance, public safety, welfare scheme implementation).

    \item \textbf{Keyword precision:} The hybrid keyword + embedding approach prioritizes precision to ensure identified complaints genuinely concern ORTPS services.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Temporal Trends and Cross-Sectional Variation}

Table~\ref{tab:ortps_aggregation} presents the fiscal year aggregation of ORTPS-related grievances, showing both temporal trends and cross-sectional variation across our pre-defined categories.

\begin{itemize}
    \item ORTPS complaints increased 75\% from FY2023 to FY2024 (24,466 $\rightarrow$ 42,904), substantially outpacing overall grievance growth.

    \item Income \& Welfare Benefits consistently dominates as the highest-volume category, comprising 4.0\% of all English complaints in FY2023, rising to 4.6\% in FY2024 and 7.2\% in FY2025 (partial year).

    \item Growth is concentrated in land-related services: Land Records (0.43\% $\rightarrow$ 0.58\%), Land Transactions (0.79\% $\rightarrow$ 1.51\%), and Land Use Changes (0.50\% $\rightarrow$ 0.67\%) all show increasing shares.

    \item \textbf{Income \& Welfare Benefits} is consistently the highest-volume category, reflecting high citizen demand for income certificates, scholarships, and ration card services.

    \item \textbf{Building \& Construction} shows a declining trend (2.10\% in FY2023 $\rightarrow$ 0.58\% in FY2024), \emph{possibly} indicating improved service delivery or reduced construction activity.

    \item \textbf{Vehicle Services} shows substantial growth (0.12\% $\rightarrow$ 0.36\%), suggesting increasing motorization and associated documentation needs.

    \item \textbf{Driving Licences} remains consistently low ($\sim$0.04--0.05\%), which could either indicate low issues or low awareness of how to seek remedies.
\end{itemize}

\section{Results: ORTPS Nature of complaints}

To understand what citizens are actually complaining about within each ORTPS category, we conducted a sample-based qualitative review of complaints across all 10 categories. This analysis reveals both the substantive issues citizens face and the limitations of text-based categorization.

\subsection{Category-Specific Complaint Patterns}

\subsubsection{Land Transactions (930 complaints, 3.9\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} This category shows the \emph{strongest thematic coherence} among all ORTPS categories. Complaints predominantly concern:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Land acquisition compensation:} Delays or non-payment of compensation for land acquired for roads, railways, and public projects (e.g., ``Non payment of land acquisition dues'', ``Payment of compensation towards acquisition of land by RD Deptt'').

    \item \textbf{Fraudulent land transactions:} Allegations of illegal or forged land transfers (e.g., ``Fraudulent and illegal transfer of land'').

    \item \textbf{Land disputes and encroachment:} Boundary disputes, illegal occupation of land, and eviction requests (e.g., ``Eviction of illegal encroachment of 221 Acrs endowment land'').

    \item \textbf{Registration delays:} Delays in property registration or document certification.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} Land Transactions complaints are well-aligned with ORTPS services (property registration, certified copies of registered documents). Minimal miscategorization observed.

\subsubsection{Land Use Changes (495 complaints, 2.1\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} Complaints focus on land use restrictions and conversion procedures:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Agricultural land conversion:} Requests or inquiries about converting agricultural land to non-agricultural use (e.g., ``I want to know about conversion of Agricultural Land to non-agricultural purpose'').

    \item \textbf{Forest land and encroachment:} Complaints about forest department restrictions on farming, requests for housing on unsurveyed forest land.

    \item \textbf{Land demarcation and development:} Delays in land development bills, demarcation requests, mutation issues.

    \item \textbf{Elephant damage:} Agricultural land damaged by wild elephants (peripheral to ORTPS but appears in this category).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} Reasonably well-aligned with ORTPS land use services (OLR Act Section 8 land conversion, mutation cases). Some peripheral issues included.

\subsubsection{Land Records (667 complaints, 2.8\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} Substantial miscategorization observed. While some complaints concern land record services, many are unrelated:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Legitimate land record issues:} Record of Rights (RoR) requests, patta demarcation, land record corrections, missing case files (e.g., ``Regarding finding out and demarcation of patta land'').

    \item \textbf{Miscategorized complaints:} Doctor transfer requests, RTI portal corrections, PM Awas housing requests (e.g., ``I AM A POOR PERSON, I HAVA NO ANY INCOME,SO I REQUEST YOUR FAVOUR TO KINDLY GRANT MY APPLICATION FOR PRADHAN MANTRI AWAS YOJANA'').
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} Mixed quality. Core land record complaints are present, but housing scheme requests and unrelated administrative issues dominate samples.

\subsubsection{Building \& Construction (2,029 complaints, 8.6\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} Heavy overlap with housing welfare schemes rather than building plan approvals:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Government building construction:} School buildings, boundary walls, GP office buildings, community centers (e.g., ``Construction of boundury wall \& kitchen of Govt. UP school'').

    \item \textbf{PM Awas Yojana requests:} Requests for housing assistance under PM Awas or Biju Pakka Ghar (e.g., ``financial assistance towards construction of house'', ``I HAVE NOT A CONCRETE ROOF HOUSE PLEASE PROVIDE A CONCRETE ROOF HOUSE'').

    \item \textbf{Infrastructure development:} Roads, tourist infrastructure, public amenities.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} \emph{Significant miscategorization}. The category conflates ORTPS building plan approval services with broader housing welfare schemes (PM Awas) and government infrastructure projects. True ORTPS building approvals (ULB/development authority building plans) are likely a minority.

\subsubsection{Identity \& Social Certificates (2,313 complaints, 9.8\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} Extremely heterogeneous category with widespread miscategorization:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Legitimate certificate issues:} Death certificate delays (e.g., ``My Father's Death certificate has been applied on April 9, 2025. It's about to be a month now, Still I haven't recieved the certificate'').

    \item \textbf{Healthcare and medicine supply:} Medicine shortages at hospitals, patient harassment (e.g., ``Following the change in government, a regularly available medicine named Factor VII used for treatment of Hemophilia A with inhibitor is seems to be unavailable'').

    \item \textbf{Corruption allegations:} Audit irregularities, gram panchayat fund misappropriation.

    \item \textbf{Housing requests:} PM Awas Yojana applications.

    \item \textbf{Infrastructure:} Temple renovation, community building repairs.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} \emph{Poor categorization quality}. While caste, birth/death, and marriage certificates are ORTPS services, the vast majority of complaints concern unrelated issues.

\subsubsection{Income \& Welfare Benefits (15,772 complaints, 66.5\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} As the largest category, it captures diverse welfare-related complaints, but with substantial noise:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Housing (PM Awas):} Dominant theme---requests for pucca houses under PM Awas Yojana or state housing schemes (e.g., ``RESPECTED SIR I SMT LATA MOHANTY W/O- BAIKUNTHA MOHANTY OF BALIAPATA LIVES WITH MY FAMILY IN AKUCHA HOUSE SO I REQUEST TO YOU TO CHECK \& ENQUARY AND GRANT A NEW PUCCA HOUSE FOR US'').

    \item \textbf{Ration cards:} New applications, member additions/deletions, corrections.

    \item \textbf{Street lights and electricity:} Infrastructure complaints about non-functional street lights (e.g., ``I am writing to you regarding the issue of installing street lights in our village'').

    \item \textbf{Income certificates and scholarships:} Likely present but not prominent in random samples, suggesting housing dominates volume.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} This category functions as a \emph{catch-all for welfare-related requests}. While income certificates, scholarships, and ration cards are core ORTPS services, the category is heavily diluted by housing scheme requests (not ORTPS) and infrastructure complaints.

\subsubsection{Utilities \& Connections (799 complaints, 3.4\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} Mixed infrastructure and administrative complaints:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Electricity connections:} Corruption allegations in new connection processes, connection delays (e.g., ``I am writing to bring to your attention a matter of grave concern...widespread corruption in the new connection process'').

    \item \textbf{Road construction:} Asphalt road requests, colony road repairs.

    \item \textbf{Administrative issues:} Data entry operator remuneration, power of attorney disputes.

    \item \textbf{Housing:} PM Awas requests.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} Partial alignment with ORTPS (piped water/electricity connections), but many complaints concern broader infrastructure or unrelated administrative matters.

\subsubsection{Driving Licences (168 complaints, 0.7\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} \emph{Severe miscategorization} observed:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Actual driving licence issues:} Learner's licence application rejections, driving test result delays, rash driving complaints (minority of samples).

    \item \textbf{Miscategorized complaints:} PM Awas housing requests, healthcare drainage issues, doctor harassment, land rights (RoR), hospital human rights violations.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} \emph{Critically poor categorization}. In a sample of 10 complaints, only 2-3 concerned actual driving licence services. The remainder were completely unrelated. This suggests the 168 complaints labeled as ``Driving Licences'' substantially overestimate true driving licence grievances.

\subsubsection{Vehicle Services (86 complaints, 0.4\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} Similar severe miscategorization:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Legitimate vehicle services:} Requests for motor vehicles for handicapped persons, vehicle payment delays for police services.

    \item \textbf{Miscategorized complaints:} Corruption allegations (gram panchayat funds), street light and bus service complaints, waste management vehicles, PM Awas housing requests, lorry owner association issues.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} \emph{Poor categorization}. True vehicle RC/ownership transfer complaints appear rare; category captures peripheral vehicle-related issues and unrelated grievances.

\subsubsection{Verification \& Legal (446 complaints, 1.9\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} Heterogeneous legal and verification complaints:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{FIR and legal protection:} Domestic violence cases, FIR registration complaints (e.g., ``Please Provide us ( me and my daughter ) Proper Justice, Safety, Security and Legal protection from Husband and In-laws'').

    \item \textbf{Verification issues:} Subhadra Yojana eligibility verification errors, fake identification complaints.

    \item \textbf{Corruption allegations:} Gram panchayat fund misappropriation.

    \item \textbf{Miscategorized:} Road construction requests, housing applications.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} Moderate alignment with ORTPS verification services, but substantial noise from infrastructure and housing complaints.

\subsection{Cross-Cutting Patterns}

\subsubsection{PM Awas Yojana Omnipresence}

The most striking cross-cutting pattern is the appearance of \textbf{PM Awas Yojana housing requests} in nearly every ORTPS category:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Building \& Construction (expected but conflated)
    \item Income \& Welfare Benefits (dominant theme)
    \item Identity \& Social Certificates
    \item Land Records
    \item Utilities \& Connections
    \item Driving Licences
    \item Vehicle Services
    \item Verification \& Legal
\end{itemize}

This pattern suggests that citizens filing housing requests use varied vocabulary that triggers false matches across multiple ORTPS categories. PM Awas is \emph{not an ORTPS service}, yet it appears to constitute a substantial fraction of complaints in several categories.

\subsubsection{Corruption and Governance Allegations}

Allegations of corruption, fund misappropriation, and administrative malpractice appear frequently across categories:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Gram panchayat audit irregularities
    \item Police harassment and inaction
    \item Electricity department bribery
    \item Document processing delays attributed to corruption
\end{itemize}

These complaints reflect broader governance issues rather than specific ORTPS service delivery bottlenecks.

\subsubsection{Infrastructure vs. Service Delivery}

Many complaints concern \textbf{infrastructure development} (roads, street lights, school buildings, boundary walls) rather than individual ORTPS service requests (certificates, licences, registrations). This conflation occurs because:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Infrastructure keywords (e.g., ``construction'', ``building'') overlap with ORTPS building plan approval terminology.
    \item Citizens may not distinguish between individual service delivery (ORTPS scope) and collective infrastructure demands.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Implications for Classification Quality}

\subsubsection{Well-Aligned Categories}

Two categories demonstrate strong alignment between complaints and ORTPS services:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Land Transactions:} Complaints concern property registration, land acquisition compensation, and document certification---core ORTPS services.
    \item \textbf{Land Use Changes:} Complaints focus on agricultural land conversion and mutation---directly aligned with ORTPS land use services.
\end{itemize}

These categories exhibit thematic coherence and minimal miscategorization.

\subsubsection{Problematic Categories}

Four categories show severe miscategorization and require improved classification:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Driving Licences (0.7\%):} Majority of complaints are unrelated (housing, healthcare, land issues). True volume likely <100 complaints.

    \item \textbf{Vehicle Services (0.4\%):} Mixed vehicle-related issues and unrelated grievances. True RC/ownership complaints are rare.

    \item \textbf{Building \& Construction (8.6\%):} Conflates ORTPS building approvals with PM Awas housing schemes and infrastructure projects. True ORTPS building plan complaints likely <2,000.

    \item \textbf{Identity \& Social Certificates (9.8\%):} Catch-all for diverse complaints (healthcare, corruption, infrastructure) with certificate issues as a minority.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Recommendations for Improved Classification}

To improve future classification accuracy:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Increase similarity threshold:} The current cosine similarity threshold of 0.45 is too permissive, allowing tangentially related complaints to match ORTPS categories. Raising the threshold to 0.60--0.70 would improve precision by filtering false matches.

    \item \textbf{Add negative keywords:} Explicitly exclude complaints containing non-ORTPS scheme names (e.g., ``PM Awas Yojana'', ``Biju Pakka Ghar'', ``MGNREGA'') from ORTPS categories unless they also contain strong positive ORTPS indicators.

    \item \textbf{Separate infrastructure from service delivery:} Create a separate ``Infrastructure \& Public Works'' category to capture road, street light, and building construction complaints that are not individual service requests.

    \item \textbf{Manual validation:} For small categories (Driving Licences, Vehicle Services), manual review of all classified complaints would identify true ORTPS grievances and improve volume estimates.
\end{enumerate}
