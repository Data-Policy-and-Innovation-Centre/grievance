\section{Data \& Methodology}

\subsection{Research Objective}

This analysis investigates the representation of Odisha Right to Public Services (ORTPS) in the state's grievance redressal system. We address two key questions:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Do ORTPS-covered services appear as distinct categories in citizen complaints submitted through the grievance redressal portal?
    \item If so, what issues do complaints related to these services typically address?
\end{enumerate}

Understanding ORTPS representation in grievance data can reveal service delivery bottlenecks, inform resource allocation decisions, and identify priority areas for process improvements.

\subsection{Methodological Challenge}

The primary challenge in identifying ORTPS-related complaints lies in the mismatch between administrative categorization and service-based classification. The existing complaint category system was not designed with the ORTPS framework in mind---categories reflect organizational structure (departments, divisions) rather than specific public services delivered to citizens.

For example, a complaint about a delayed caste certificate might be categorized under the Education category administratively, since a caste certificate may be needed to avail reserved seats in colleges and universities, but from a service delivery perspective, it falls under the ORTPS framework's identity documentation services. This misalignment necessitates an alternative approach to identify ORTPS-related complaints based on the complaint text itself rather than pre-assigned categories.

\subsection{ORTPS Category Consolidation}

We began with the full list of ORTPS-covered services as provided by CAGR and consolidated them into 10 issue-based categories covering major service domains. This consolidation balances specificity with analytical tractability, grouping related services that citizens and administrators perceive as similar types of requests.

The 10 consolidated categories are:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Identity \& Social Certificates} --- Caste certificates (SC/ST/OBC/SEBC), birth and death certificates, marriage registration, legal heir certificates

    \item \textbf{Income \& Welfare Benefits} --- Income certificates, Income \& Asset certificates (EWS), scholarships (pre-matric, post-matric, national/merit/minority schemes), ration cards (AAY, BPL, PHH, food security)

    \item \textbf{Land Records} --- Certified copies of Record of Rights (RoR), encumbrance certificates

    \item \textbf{Land Transactions} --- Property registration documents, certified copies of registered documents, mortgage permissions, conveyance deeds

    \item \textbf{Land Use Changes} --- Disposal of uncontested mutation cases, mutation orders for leasehold land, land conversion under OLR Act Section 8, partition of land under OLR Act Section 19

    \item \textbf{Building \& Construction} --- Building plan approvals by Urban Local Bodies (ULBs)/development authorities, permissions for building additions/alterations, fire safety recommendations and certificates

    \item \textbf{Utilities \& Connections} --- Piped water connections (BMC, CMC, BEMC), new electricity connections (non-industrial)

    \item \textbf{Driving Licences} --- Issuance of driving licences, licence renewals, change of address in driving licence, learner's licences

    \item \textbf{Vehicle Services} --- Certified copies of vehicle registration certificates (RC), transfer of vehicle ownership

    \item \textbf{Verification \& Legal} --- Employee/character/antecedent verification requests, certified copies of FIR, trade licences (provisional and final)
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Text-Based Classification Pipeline}

Given the administrative categorization challenge, we developed a two-stage automated classification pipeline operating directly on complaint text.

\subsubsection{Language Detection}

To ensure reliable keyword matching, we first filter complaints to English-language text using a two-stage detection approach:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Script Detection:} A regex-based filter identifies and excludes complaints containing Odia or Devanagari (Hindi) scripts, which are detectable by their Unicode character ranges.

    \item \textbf{Lingua Confidence Scoring:} For complaints passing the script filter, we use the Lingua language detection library to compute English-language confidence scores. Complaints with confidence $\geq 0.85$ are classified as English and retained for further analysis.
\end{enumerate}

This two-stage approach balances precision and recall, filtering out non-English text while preserving code-mixed complaints where English is dominant.

\subsubsection{Category Labeling}

For English-language complaints, we employ a hybrid classification approach combining keyword matching and semantic similarity:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Keyword Matching:} Case-insensitive substring matching against category-specific keyword lists. For example, ``Income \& Welfare Benefits'' includes keywords such as \textit{income certificate}, \textit{scholarship}, \textit{post matric}, \textit{ration card}, \textit{bpl card}, and related terms. This method captures complaints with explicit mentions of service names.

    \item \textbf{Embedding Similarity:} Complaints not matched by keywords are evaluated using sentence embeddings (all-MiniLM-L6-v2 model) to compute semantic similarity to category prototypes. Complaints with cosine similarity $\geq 0.60$ to any category are assigned to that category. This method captures semantically related complaints that may not use exact service terminology (e.g., ``need money certificate for college admission'' $\rightarrow$ Income \& Welfare Benefits).
\end{enumerate}

Complaints matching neither method remain unlabeled, indicating they likely concern non-ORTPS services (e.g., infrastructure, law and order, general administration).

\subsection{Data Scope}

We restrict our analysis along two dimensions:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Temporal scope:} July 2023 onwards (fiscal years 2023--2025). This recent time window reflects current service delivery patterns and ensures sufficient data volume for category-level analysis.

    \item \textbf{Language filter:} English-only complaints. Since our ORTPS keyword lists were developed in English, language filtering ensures classification reliability. While this excludes Odia-language complaints, English remains the predominant language in the portal (over 70\% of total complaints).
\end{itemize}

The final analysis dataset comprises \textbf{763,237 English-language complaints} from July 2023 onwards.

\section{Results: ORTPS Representation}

\input{fiscal_year_aggregation_wide.tex}

Of 763,237 English-language complaints analyzed, \textbf{58,813 complaints (6.0\%)} were identified as ORTPS-related using our classification pipeline with a cosine similarity threshold of 0.60.

This conservative match rate reflects the focused, service-specific nature of ORTPS and the higher precision threshold employed to minimize false positives. The grievance portal captures a broad range of citizen concerns---infrastructure quality, law and order issues, general administrative queries, and non-ORTPS government services---of which ORTPS-covered services constitute one important but bounded subset.

The 6.0\% match rate should be interpreted as indicating:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Focused categorization:} Our classification correctly identifies a well-defined subset of complaints rather than over-classifying unrelated complaints as ORTPS-related.

    \item \textbf{Service portfolio breadth:} Government service delivery extends well beyond ORTPS-covered services. Many complaints concern services outside ORTPS scope (e.g., infrastructure maintenance, public safety, welfare scheme implementation).

    \item \textbf{Keyword precision:} The hybrid keyword + embedding approach prioritizes precision to ensure identified complaints genuinely concern ORTPS services.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Temporal Trends and Cross-Sectional Variation}

Table~\ref{tab:ortps_aggregation} presents the fiscal year aggregation of ORTPS-related grievances, showing both temporal trends and cross-sectional variation across our pre-defined categories.

Key patterns observed with the higher precision threshold (0.60):

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Income \& Welfare Benefits} dominates ORTPS complaints, consistently representing the highest volume across fiscal years. This reflects high citizen demand for income certificates, scholarships, and ration card services, though housing welfare requests (PM Awas) contribute substantial noise to this category.

    \item \textbf{Land-related services} show sustained demand across Land Records, Land Transactions, and Land Use Changes categories. These categories exhibit stronger thematic coherence and alignment with ORTPS services compared to other categories.

    \item \textbf{Building \& Construction} volumes declined significantly with the higher threshold, indicating that the previous 0.45 threshold captured substantial false positives (primarily PM Awas housing requests and infrastructure projects). The higher threshold filters these miscategorizations more effectively.

    \item \textbf{Small categories} (Driving Licences, Vehicle Services) show dramatically reduced volumes with the higher threshold, suggesting that most previously classified complaints in these categories were false positives. True ORTPS-related driving licence and vehicle registration complaints appear to be rare.

    \item \textbf{Identity \& Social Certificates} maintains moderate volume, though sample review reveals heterogeneous content including healthcare, corruption allegations, and infrastructure alongside legitimate certificate requests.
\end{itemize}

\section{Results: ORTPS Nature of complaints}

To understand what citizens are actually complaining about within each ORTPS category, we conducted a sample-based qualitative review of complaints across all 10 categories. This analysis reveals both the substantive issues citizens face and the limitations of text-based categorization.

\subsection{Category-Specific Complaint Patterns}

\subsubsection{Land Transactions (5,069 complaints, 8.6\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} This category shows the \emph{strongest thematic coherence} among all ORTPS categories. Complaints predominantly concern:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Land acquisition compensation:} Delays or non-payment of compensation for land acquired for roads, railways, and public projects (e.g., ``Non payment of land acquisition dues'', ``Payment of compensation towards acquisition of land by RD Deptt'').

    \item \textbf{Fraudulent land transactions:} Allegations of illegal or forged land transfers (e.g., ``Fraudulent and illegal transfer of land'').

    \item \textbf{Land disputes and encroachment:} Boundary disputes, illegal occupation of land, and eviction requests (e.g., ``Eviction of illegal encroachment of 221 Acrs endowment land'').

    \item \textbf{Registration delays:} Delays in property registration or document certification.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} Land Transactions complaints are well-aligned with ORTPS services (property registration, certified copies of registered documents). Minimal miscategorization observed.

\subsubsection{Land Use Changes (2,487 complaints, 4.2\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} Complaints focus on land use restrictions and conversion procedures:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Agricultural land conversion:} Requests or inquiries about converting agricultural land to non-agricultural use (e.g., ``I want to know about conversion of Agricultural Land to non-agricultural purpose'').

    \item \textbf{Forest land and encroachment:} Complaints about forest department restrictions on farming, requests for housing on unsurveyed forest land.

    \item \textbf{Land demarcation and development:} Delays in land development bills, demarcation requests, mutation issues.

    \item \textbf{Elephant damage:} Agricultural land damaged by wild elephants (peripheral to ORTPS but appears in this category).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} Reasonably well-aligned with ORTPS land use services (OLR Act Section 8 land conversion, mutation cases). Some peripheral issues included.

\subsubsection{Land Records (2,346 complaints, 4.0\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} Substantial miscategorization observed. Topic modeling on 214 sampled complaints identified 2 topics:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Topic 0 (102 complaints):} Audit/corruption allegations, FIR reports for stolen phones, and some legitimate RoR mutation errors (e.g., ``I apply mutation for inheritance online at banki tahasil...but in ror there is a big mistake the father name is diffrent not as per legalhire'').

    \item \textbf{Topic 1 (81 complaints):} PM Awas housing requests, Subhadra Yojana applications, community mandap construction, school classroom construction.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} Mixed quality with severe noise. While legitimate land record complaints (RoR, mutation, patta demarcation) are present, the category is heavily diluted by PM Awas requests and unrelated complaints (stolen phone FIRs, corruption allegations, infrastructure construction). Estimate: <50\% concern actual ORTPS land record services.

\subsubsection{Building \& Construction (1,109 complaints, 1.9\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} With the higher threshold, this category contracted significantly from previous analyses (from 8.6\% to 1.9\%), filtering out many false positives. Topic modeling on 101 sampled complaints identified 2 topics, both showing heavy overlap with housing welfare schemes and public infrastructure rather than individual building plan approvals:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Topic 0 (54 complaints):} Audit/corruption allegations and PM Awas Yojana requests (e.g., ``I am a Sc \& B.P.L Family holder and I Have not a Singel House...Therefor I may be Provided PRADHAN MANTRI AWAS OR BIJU PAKKA GHARA''), environmental complaints about plastic pollution.

    \item \textbf{Topic 1 (47 complaints):} Public infrastructure construction projects---market complexes, CHC buildings, school renovations, community halls (e.g., ``CONSTRUCTION OF ROAD, MARKET COMPLEX, GUARD WALL, COMMUNITY HALL, CHECK DAM'').
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} \emph{Significant miscategorization}. The category conflates ORTPS building plan approval services with broader housing welfare schemes (PM Awas) and government infrastructure projects. True ORTPS building approvals (ULB/development authority building plans) for individual citizens are likely fewer than 10\% of categorized complaints.

\subsubsection{Identity \& Social Certificates (5,638 complaints, 9.6\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} Extremely heterogeneous category with widespread miscategorization. Topic modeling on 2,245 sampled complaints identified 11 topics, with the top 2 topics revealing severe noise:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Topic 0 (401 complaints):} Predominantly PM Awas Yojana housing requests (e.g., ``I am BHAGBAI AHIRWAR...I write this letter to request for a Biju Pucca Ghar Yojana'', ``I AM VERY POOR. PLEASE ENLIST MY NAME IN THE YEAR 2024-25 PMAY AWAS YOJONA'').

    \item \textbf{Topic 1 (213 complaints):} Systematic audit corruption allegations using identical template language (e.g., ``An allegation of Corruption against the delinquent employees working in [GP NAME]...for misappropriation \& irregularities in govt. aided money in an audit report''). These appear to be mass-filed RTI-based corruption complaints, not certificate requests.

    \item \textbf{Legitimate certificate issues:} Death certificate delays, caste certificate requests (present in lower-volume topics, but not dominant).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} \emph{Poor categorization quality}. While caste, birth/death, and marriage certificates are ORTPS services, the category is dominated by PM Awas requests (Topic 0) and systematic corruption allegations (Topic 1). True certificate complaints appear to constitute less than 20\% of the category.

\subsubsection{Income \& Welfare Benefits (38,779 complaints, 65.9\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} As the largest category by far (nearly two-thirds of all ORTPS-labeled complaints), it captures diverse welfare-related complaints but with substantial noise. Topic modeling on 15,755 sampled complaints identified 32 topics, with housing schemes dominating:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Topic 0 (3,004 complaints):} PM Awas Yojana housing requests in English (e.g., ``I am BRUNDABATI BEHERA...I have applied PM AWAS YOJANA through offline mode but so far am exempted from this facility'').

    \item \textbf{Topic 1 (1,017 complaints):} PM Awas housing requests in Odia language (keywords: ghara, mora, mote, kari).

    \item \textbf{Topic 2 (509 complaints):} Kisan Subhadra Yojana and ration card complaints.

    \item \textbf{Income certificates and scholarships:} Present in lower-volume topics but not prominent in top 3, suggesting housing dominates volume by 4:1 margin or greater.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} This category functions as a \emph{catch-all for welfare-related requests}. While income certificates, scholarships, and ration cards are core ORTPS services, the category is heavily diluted by housing scheme requests (PM Awas and Biju Pakka Ghar---not ORTPS services). Conservative estimate: fewer than 25\% of complaints concern actual ORTPS welfare services.

\subsubsection{Utilities \& Connections (2,055 complaints, 3.5\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} Mixed infrastructure and administrative complaints with severe data quality issues. Topic modeling on 808 sampled complaints identified 3 topics:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Topic 0 (667 complaints):} Highly anomalous content---spam, mental health complaints, and incoherent text (keywords: ``house'', ``action'', ``abracadabra'', ``line''). Sample includes extended rambling about religious figures, invisible forces, and conspiracy theories. This suggests either bot-generated spam or mental health crisis complaints that passed language filters.

    \item \textbf{Topics 1-2:} Road construction requests, electricity connections, and infrastructure development.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} \emph{Critical data quality issues}. The dominant topic (Topic 0, 83\% of sampled complaints) contains spam or severely incoherent content. Even after filtering for this, legitimate ORTPS utility connection complaints (piped water, electricity) appear rare. Estimate: <10\% concern actual ORTPS-covered utility services.

\subsubsection{Driving Licences (395 complaints, 0.7\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} \emph{Severe miscategorization} observed across 2 identified topics:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Topic 0 (49 complaints):} Almost entirely PM Awas housing requests (e.g., ``I am a poor, having a Kacca House, facing many more problem in this season. So kindly allow me a PMAY'').

    \item \textbf{Topic 1 (31 complaints):} Mixed content including rare legitimate driving licence complaints (e.g., RTO corruption in address correction: ``for adress changing in my driving licence...RTO staff they will forcefully demand me Rs 300 for Red cross donation''), but primarily electricity issues, audit corruption allegations, and generic action requests.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} \emph{Critically poor categorization}. Topic modeling on 160 sampled complaints identified only 2 topics, with the dominant topic being entirely PM Awas requests. Legitimate driving licence complaints appear to constitute fewer than 10\% of the category. This suggests the 395 complaints labeled as ``Driving Licences'' substantially overestimate true driving licence grievances by a factor of 10 or more.

\subsubsection{Vehicle Services (233 complaints, 0.4\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} Similar severe miscategorization:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Legitimate vehicle services:} Requests for motor vehicles for handicapped persons, vehicle payment delays for police services.

    \item \textbf{Miscategorized complaints:} Corruption allegations (gram panchayat funds), street light and bus service complaints, waste management vehicles, PM Awas housing requests, lorry owner association issues.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} \emph{Poor categorization}. True vehicle RC/ownership transfer complaints appear rare; category captures peripheral vehicle-related issues and unrelated grievances.

\subsubsection{Verification \& Legal (702 complaints, 1.2\%)}

\textbf{Primary themes:} Heterogeneous legal and verification complaints. Topic modeling on 279 sampled complaints identified 4 topics:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Topic 0 (86 complaints):} Systematic audit corruption allegations using template language, illegal business/trade license complaints, CC drain construction disputes.

    \item \textbf{Topic 1 (61 complaints):} Pension applications (NIGOAP), bus harassment complaints, government grant-in-aid (GIA) sanctions, office transfer/rotation policy violations, university degree equivalency disputes.

    \item \textbf{Topic 2 (47 complaints):} PM Awas housing requests in Odia language, school classroom construction.

    \item \textbf{Topics 3:} Residual mixed content.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Assessment:} Moderate alignment with ORTPS verification services, but substantial noise. While some complaints concern document verification and legal attestation (ORTPS services), the category is diluted by corruption allegations (Topic 0), pension/administrative issues (Topic 1), and housing requests (Topic 2). Estimate: <30\% concern actual ORTPS verification services.

\subsection{Cross-Cutting Patterns}

\subsubsection{PM Awas Yojana Omnipresence}

The most striking cross-cutting pattern revealed by topic modeling is the appearance of \textbf{PM Awas Yojana housing requests as Topic 0 or Topic 1} in nearly every ORTPS category:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Income \& Welfare Benefits:} Topic 0 (3,004 complaints, largest single topic) and Topic 1 (1,017 Odia-language housing requests)
    \item \textbf{Identity \& Social Certificates:} Topic 0 (401 complaints, largest single topic)
    \item \textbf{Driving Licences:} Topic 0 (49 complaints out of 80 total in top 2 topics)
    \item \textbf{Building \& Construction:} Major component of Topic 0
    \item Also present in: Land Records, Utilities \& Connections, Vehicle Services, Verification \& Legal
\end{itemize}

This systematic pattern suggests that citizens filing housing requests use varied vocabulary (``house'', ``awas'', ``pucca ghar'', ``shelter'', ``construction'') that triggers false matches across multiple ORTPS categories. \textbf{PM Awas is not an ORTPS service}, yet it appears to constitute 30-60\% of complaints in several categories. This represents the single largest source of classification error in the current pipeline.

\subsubsection{Corruption and Governance Allegations}

Allegations of corruption, fund misappropriation, and administrative malpractice appear frequently across categories:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Gram panchayat audit irregularities
    \item Police harassment and inaction
    \item Electricity department bribery
    \item Document processing delays attributed to corruption
\end{itemize}

These complaints reflect broader governance issues rather than specific ORTPS service delivery bottlenecks.

\subsubsection{Infrastructure vs. Service Delivery}

Many complaints concern \textbf{infrastructure development} (roads, street lights, school buildings, boundary walls) rather than individual ORTPS service requests (certificates, licences, registrations). This conflation occurs because:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Infrastructure keywords (e.g., ``construction'', ``building'') overlap with ORTPS building plan approval terminology.
    \item Citizens may not distinguish between individual service delivery (ORTPS scope) and collective infrastructure demands.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Implications for Classification Quality}

\subsubsection{Well-Aligned Categories}

Two categories demonstrate strong alignment between complaints and ORTPS services:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Land Transactions:} Complaints concern property registration, land acquisition compensation, and document certification---core ORTPS services.
    \item \textbf{Land Use Changes:} Complaints focus on agricultural land conversion and mutation---directly aligned with ORTPS land use services.
\end{itemize}

These categories exhibit thematic coherence and minimal miscategorization.

\subsubsection{Problematic Categories}

Four categories show severe miscategorization and require improved classification:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Driving Licences (0.7\%, 395 complaints):} Majority of complaints are unrelated PM Awas housing requests (Topic 0: 49 complaints). True volume likely <40 complaints (<10\% of category).

    \item \textbf{Vehicle Services (0.4\%, 233 complaints):} Mixed vehicle-related issues and unrelated grievances. True RC/ownership transfer complaints are rare.

    \item \textbf{Building \& Construction (1.9\%, 1,109 complaints):} Conflates ORTPS building approvals with PM Awas housing schemes (Topic 0) and public infrastructure projects (Topic 1). True ORTPS individual building plan complaints likely <100 (<10\% of category).

    \item \textbf{Identity \& Social Certificates (9.6\%, 5,638 complaints):} Catch-all dominated by PM Awas requests (Topic 0: 401 complaints) and systematic corruption allegations (Topic 1: 213 complaints). True certificate complaints likely <1,000 (<20\% of category).
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Recommendations for Improved Classification}

To further improve classification accuracy:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Higher threshold implemented:} We increased the cosine similarity threshold from 0.45 to 0.60, reducing false positives and improving precision. The trade-off is lower recall---some legitimate ORTPS complaints may now be excluded---but classification quality has improved for well-aligned categories.

    \item \textbf{Add negative keywords:} Explicitly exclude complaints containing non-ORTPS scheme names (e.g., ``PM Awas Yojana'', ``Biju Pakka Ghar'', ``MGNREGA'') from ORTPS categories unless they also contain strong positive ORTPS indicators. This would address the PM Awas omnipresence problem observed across categories.

    \item \textbf{Separate infrastructure from service delivery:} Create a separate ``Infrastructure \& Public Works'' category to capture road, street light, and building construction complaints that are not individual service requests. This would reduce noise in Building \& Construction and Utilities categories.

    \item \textbf{Manual validation:} For small categories (Driving Licences, Vehicle Services, Verification \& Legal), manual review of all classified complaints would identify true ORTPS grievances and improve volume estimates. Given the severe miscategorization observed, human validation is essential for these categories.

    \item \textbf{Iterative threshold tuning:} Consider category-specific thresholds rather than a global threshold. Well-defined services like Land Transactions could use higher thresholds (0.65--0.70), while broader services like Income \& Welfare Benefits may require lower thresholds (0.55) to maintain adequate recall.
\end{enumerate}
